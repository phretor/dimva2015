Submission

Before and while submitting a paper to DIMVA, please make sure to read the following instructions.

Paper length and content

Also this year, DIMVA accepts full or short papers. In either case, the research contribution must be novel and not substantially overlapping with work that has been published before, or that is simultaneously under submission.

Full papers

Must present novel and mature research results, are limited to 20 pages, prepared according to the instructions provided below. They will be reviewed by the program committee, and papers accepted for presentation at the conference will be included in the proceedings.

Short papers EXTENDED ABSTRACTS

Must present original, still ongoing work that has not yet reached the maturity required for a full paper, are limited to 10 pages, prepared according to the instructions provided below. They will also be reviewed by the program committee, and papers accepted for presentation at the conference will be included in the proceedings (containing Extended Abstract in the title).

Formatting rules

Submitted papers must be in English and must not substantially overlap work that has been published before, or that is simultaneously in submission to a journal or a conference with proceedings. Simultaneous submission, submission of previously published work, and plagiarism constitute dishonesty or fraud. DIMVA prohibits these practices and may take appropriate action against authors who have committed them.

Authors' names

Submissions must not be double blind, (i.e., authors information must appear in the paper).

Ethical aspects

Authors are encouraged to consider ethical aspects of their research and the impact of publication of their work.

Submission and proceedings

All papers must be submitted electronically in PDF format via the EasyChair system.

The conference proceedings will be published in the Springer LNCS series. Submissions must be formatted in LNCS style according to the instructions provided by Springer Verlag.

Presentation

Authors of accepted papers must ensure that their papers will be presented at the conference. Presentations must also be held in English.
